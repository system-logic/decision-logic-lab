# Decision Logic Lab Manifesto

## Scope
This is a research log of architectural and decision-making thinking in real-world contexts.  
Not education, not prescription, not generalization.

## What This Is
- Public record of reasoning in specific situations  
- Demonstration of how decisions form, break, and get revised  
- Archive of structures, boundaries, constraints

## What This Is NOT
- Tutorial / course / best practices / templates / advice  
If you leave thinking "now I know how to build this" — failure.

## Responsibility Boundary
Understanding does not equal permission to apply.  
Every example is valid only in its original context.  
Using it elsewhere = full responsibility on you.

## Tone
No universal claims. No "one should". No better/worse comparisons.

## Final Test
Desired takeaway:  
"I understand the structure — and I understand that applying it is a responsibility I must consciously accept."
